**Metatron Dynamics — Internal Strategy Document**

## Metatron's Position

Metatron Dynamics is not a hardware manufacturer. Metatron is the **system integrator and domain governance layer** for modular anthropogenic processing.

Every company listed below manufactures one type of module. No single entity consistently integrates them. Nobody declares the local feedstock domain, configures the appropriate module combination, and enforces operational boundaries across the integrated system. That is the gap Metatron fills — and it maps directly to the Origin role applied at the business architecture level.

Metatron's value proposition to each partner: **we bring you deployments you wouldn't otherwise get**, because we solve the configuration and governance problem that stands between their module and a customer's specific site.

The following companies represent potential strategic alignment targets based on publicly available information and observed operational fit. No commercial relationships or endorsements are implied.

## Tier 1 — Priority US Partners (Highest Strategic Alignment)

### Pyrolysis: Resynergi
**Location:** California (relocating from Rohnert Park for scale-up)
**Why they matter:** Resynergi has developed systems that closely align with the MAPD concept — a patented Continuous Microwave Assisted Pyrolysis (CMAP) system that fits on an 18-wheeler truck. Their tagline is "No factory or facility required = fast, low risk deployment." They bring the system to the plastic waste at Material Recovery Facilities. Their manufacturing partner is Lummus, a global leader in process technology since 1907.

**Strategic fit:** Resynergi has the mobile pyrolysis unit. Metatron has the domain declaration and configuration framework. Together: Metatron characterizes the local feedstock (Operator A), Resynergi deploys the matched processing unit, and the ABRCE framework governs runtime operations. Resynergi is currently seeking deployment partners — they have a waitlist for CMAP system purchases and operator partnerships.

**Action:** Direct outreach. Their decentralized model is the MAPD pyrolysis module.

### Pyrolysis: Nexus Circular
**Location:** Atlanta, Georgia
**Why they matter:** Nexus Circular is ISCC PLUS certified and operates at commercial scale, converting landfill-bound plastics (HDPE, LDPE, PP, PS — the same four polyolefin types the MAPD sorts for) into liquid hydrocarbons for virgin-quality new plastics. They accept feedstock without costly pre-treatment, have diverted millions of pounds from landfill, and their output exceeds stringent customer quality requirements without post-treatment.

**Strategic fit:** Nexus has proprietary pyrolysis technology optimized for the exact feedstock streams the MAPD sorting module produces. They need characterized, sorted feedstock — which is precisely what the MAPD Module 1 delivers. Partnership model: Metatron deploys sorting + characterization depots that feed Nexus processing facilities, or co-locates Nexus processing with MAPD sorting at larger sites.

**Action:** Explore feedstock supply partnership. Metatron's sorting depots become Nexus's upstream supply chain.

### Sorting: BHS / National Recovery Technologies (NRT)
**Location:** Eugene, Oregon (BHS) / Nashville, Tennessee (NRT)
**Why they matter:** BHS is the leading US-based recycling systems integrator. NRT holds 35+ patents in optical sorting and their SpydIR-HS system offers hyperspectral imaging at nearly 10x standard detection resolution. In January 2026, BHS opened a new customer testing and evaluation center in Nashville for real-time material stream testing. They design complete MRF systems from engineering through installation.

**Strategic fit:** NRT/BHS provides the Operator A hardware — the sorting/characterization technology that declares the feedstock domain. Their systems are modular and can be configured for different waste streams. Partnership model: Metatron specifies the sorting configuration based on site characterization (domain declaration), BHS/NRT supplies and installs the sorting module, and the MAPD framework governs the integrated system.

**Action:** Technical partnership for Module 1 specification and supply. BHS's Nashville test center could validate MAPD sorting configurations.

### Metals: Inductotherm Corporation
**Location:** Rancocas, New Jersey

They cover the full range from small crucible furnaces to industrial scale, processing aluminum, copper, steel, zinc, precious metals, and specialty alloys. ## Partnership Architecture

In every partnership, Metatron's role is consistent:

| Function | Metatron's Role | Partner's Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Site Characterization** (Operator A) | Declares the feedstock domain — composition, volume, contamination profile, seasonal variation | Provides site access and operational data |
| **Configuration Selection** (Operators B + R) | Specifies module combination matched to declared domain; defines relational structure between modules | Supplies specified hardware modules |
| **Pre-Deployment Derivation** (Operator C) | Derives operational properties — break-even, energy balance, output mix, scaling limits — before deployment | Validates technical specifications |
| **Runtime Governance** (Operator E) | Provides enforcement framework for domain closure during operation | Operates and maintains equipment |

Metatron does not compete with any of these companies. Metatron makes their products deployable with structural guarantees they cannot currently offer alone.

## What We're Not Doing

We are not building hardware. We are not competing with pyrolysis manufacturers, sorting equipment companies, or furnace suppliers. We are not a waste management company.

We are the entity that declares the domain, configures the system, and enforces the boundaries — so that every partner's technology operates within its valid operating envelope, every deployment is matched to its actual feedstock, and every network node is independently viable and structurally governed.

That is what an Origin does — the role responsible for declaring the domain and ensuring system validity. Applied to an industry.
